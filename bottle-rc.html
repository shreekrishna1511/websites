Despite the unconventional fuselage material, the Bottle RC Plane is designed with rigorous aerodynamic principles. The NACA 6412 airfoil provides the necessary high-lift characteristics to support the heavier PET chassis at low speeds.

Engineering Challenges

1. Structural Rigidity

Joining cylindrical bottles while maintaining a straight thrust line required developing a custom heat-shrink jig to ensure perfect alignment.

2. Weight Management

The PET fuselage is heavier than balsa. We optimized wing loading by extending the span to 1200mm and utilizing a high-lift airfoil.

3. CG Balancing

The heavy plastic tail section required precise placement of the LiPo battery in the nose bottle to achieve the correct Center of Gravity (25-30% MAC).
